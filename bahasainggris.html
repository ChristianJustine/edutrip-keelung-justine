Discover Taiwan Through English

Taiwan offers a fascinating blend of culture, history, and cuisine that provides excellent material for English learning. By exploring Taiwan's unique characteristics in English, you can improve your language skills while learning about this beautiful island.

Did You Know?

Taiwan's night markets are among the best places to practice English while enjoying delicious food. Many vendors speak basic English and are happy to converse with international visitors.

Bubble tea, now popular worldwide, originated in Taiwan in the 1980s

Taiwan has over 300 night markets, each with its own specialties

The term "stinky tofu" was first translated by English-speaking visitors to Taiwan

Taiwan's Culinary Delights

In Taiwan, people from all over can try many interesting and unique foods. We can try oyster omelets, stinky tofu, street steak, and more. People from all over the world can try those foods in famous night markets or Old Streets. One of the Old Streets is Jiufen Old Street. The food in Old Street offers us a unique taste of Taiwan, and every food in Jiufen old street can have a history behind it.

Keelung Night Market - One of Taiwan's most famous food destinations
Jiufen Old Street - A historic street with traditional Taiwanese snacks
Stinky Tofu - Taiwan's most famous (and smelly) delicacy

Food Vocabulary

Oyster Omelet (蚵仔煎)

A popular Taiwanese snack made with eggs, oysters, and starch.

Pronunciation: /ˈɔɪ.stər ˈɒm.ə.leɪ/

Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐)

Fermented tofu with a strong odor but delicious taste.

Pronunciation: /ˈstɪŋ.ki ˈtoʊ.fuː/

Bubble Tea (珍珠奶茶)

Sweet tea drink with chewy tapioca pearls.

Pronunciation: /ˈbʌb.əl tiː/

Gua Bao (刈包)

Taiwanese hamburger with pork belly in a steamed bun.

Pronunciation: /ɡwɑː baʊ/

The Discovery of Taiwan's Seafood

Taiwan is famous for its fresh and delicious seafood. We can discover new seafood places in almost every corner. One of the famous seafood places I tried is in Lukang Old Street. Even though they serve seafood dishes, the restaurant looks like a Chinese restaurant. In Taiwan, many seafood restaurants serve food in a Chinese restaurant way, served at a round table.

Oyster Omelet - A classic Taiwanese seafood dish

Exploring Taiwan's Street Food

Taiwan is also famous for its street food. Taiwan is not only famous for the Chinese food or the fishy seafood, it is also famous for the street food in the night markets. Street food is food that is served in the street. Taiwan's street food isn't only food from Taiwan, it also has food from all over the world. One of the places you can try street food is in Fengchia Night Market. Fengchia Night Market lets you explore endless street food.

Night Market Phrases

How much is this?

這個多少錢？(Zhège duōshǎo qián?)

Essential for bargaining at night markets

One more, please

請再來一個 (Qǐng zài lái yīgè)

When you want to order more

Too spicy!

太辣了！(Tài làle!)

Important if you can't handle spicy food
